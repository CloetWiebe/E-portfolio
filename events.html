Open House MARELEC

MARELEC Food Technologies designs and manufactures high-tech portioning, weighing, grading and control systems for the fishing and food industry, to optimize the efficiency of the manufacturing process. It is a company that is situated in Nieuwpoort, Belgium. Really close to where I live. It seemed like a great opportunity to connect with the local IT industry in my home town.

It was truly eye-opening to witness just how deeply technology is integrated into the food industry. I got an up-close look at the impressive range of machines they use, from precision portioning systems to advanced water-jet cutters for meat, and even their in-house developed sorting machines. Each piece of equipment demonstrated how innovation drives efficiency in this field. On top of that, I secured a summer job there, which will gave me the opportunity to further explore the fascinating intersection of IT and the food industry.

Summer Job MARELEC

For the active portion of these events, I will be sharing some of my experiences working at MARELEC. As I mentioned in my previous presentation, it was truly eye-opening to see just how integral technology is to the food industry. During my summer at MARELEC, I had the unique opportunity to get hands-on experience with their cutting-edge equipment. My tasks varied widely, from software testing to operating actual machinery, providing me with valuable insight into both the technical and practical aspects of the industry.

Of course, there were also more tedious tasks, such as cataloging electronic components. In fact, I counted over 340 different types of components and tracked how many of each were on hand. Despite the repetitive nature of this work, it gave me a greater appreciation for the meticulous attention to detail that goes into building these complex systems.

One of the most rewarding challenges I faced was developing a proof of concept using an inductive sensor to detect the full extension of a piston. The program I created had to manage parameters such as extension and retraction times, as well as the duration of the entire cycle. This involved writing code in TwinCAT 3 Structured Text for a PLC, which then communicated with a Python script. The script handled command-line inputs, displayed whether the process succeeded or failed, and logged any information about failed cycles. It took me about two weeks to complete this challenge. Although Structured Text programming was new to me, it was an incredibly valuable learning experience.

Overall, my time at MARELEC was fantastic. I learned a tremendous amount from the team, and the experience has left a lasting impact on my understanding of both technology and the food industry.
